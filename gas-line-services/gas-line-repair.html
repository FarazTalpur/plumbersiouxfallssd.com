Frequently Asked Questions

Signs that your gas line may need repair include a rotten egg smell, hissing sounds near the gas line, a significant increase in your gas bill, and dead or dying vegetation around the gas line area.

If you suspect a gas leak, immediately evacuate the area and call your gas company's emergency number or 911. Do not use electrical devices, smoke, or light any flames.

It is highly recommended to hire a licensed professional for gas line repairs. Attempting to fix a gas line yourself can be extremely dangerous and may lead to further damage or leaks.

Typically, gas lines should be inspected every year to ensure they are in good condition. Regular inspections can help prevent leaks and other issues.

Common causes include incorrect installation, physical damage from construction or digging, corrosion, and natural wear and tear over time.
