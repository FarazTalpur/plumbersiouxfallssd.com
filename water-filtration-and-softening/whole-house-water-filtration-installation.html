Frequently Asked Questions

A whole-house water filtration system provides cleaner, safer water throughout your entire home by removing contaminants such as chlorine, sediment, and other harmful substances. It improves water quality for drinking, bathing, and cleaning, and can extend the life of household appliances by preventing scale buildup.

To choose the right system, consider factors like the size of your home, the water quality in your area, and your specific water needs. It's also helpful to consult with a water treatment professional who can perform a water test and recommend the best system based on your circumstances.

Installation typically takes a few hours, depending on the complexity of the plumbing and the specific system being installed. A professional can provide a more accurate estimate based on your home's layout and the system selected.

Yes, regular maintenance is necessary to ensure optimal performance. This includes periodic replacement of filters and media, which varies depending on the system type and the quality of your water. Some systems also require an annual inspection by a professional.

While it is possible for a skilled DIYer to install a system, it's generally recommended to hire a professional. Proper installation is crucial to ensure the system works efficiently and to avoid potential issues with plumbing or leaks.
